Research systems for technical writing

LatexDo Research

We study the machinery behind reliable LaTeX workflows: compiler intelligence, document knowledge graphs, and AI systems that can reason over source, citations, figures, and review history.

Compiler Knowledge Graph AI
01

Compiler

Faster project analysis, better diagnostics, SyncTeX-aware feedback, and reproducible local builds for papers that need to move from draft to submission without losing trust.

02

Knowledge Graph

Structured maps of citations, claims, equations, sections, figures, reviewers, and rebuttal context so research documents become inspectable systems instead of disconnected files.

03

AI

Assistants that work from grounded project context: explaining compiler errors, checking paper consistency, preparing rebuttals, and surfacing missing evidence without taking over authorship.

Organization

Research that feeds the product.

LatexDo Research is the experimental edge of the LatexDo organization. The work here is practical: prototypes become editor features, evaluation suites, compiler improvements, and public notes when they are ready.
